Q4b — POSSIBLE or PROBABLE for L7-S3 in pelvic monoparesis?

What the tiers mean in our system

Each rule in the localisation engine emits one of three confidence tiers — DEFINITE, PROBABLE, POSSIBLE — for every localisation it fires. They map to how strongly the engine is asserting that localisation given the parameters in front of it:

  • DEFINITE — parameters fit cleanly, no meaningful alternative.
  • PROBABLE — strong fit, but at least one other localisation could plausibly produce the same picture; the vet should weigh it as a real differential alongside the DEFINITE one.
  • POSSIBLE — kept on the table because it cannot be ruled out, but the case more likely points elsewhere; the vet would investigate the higher-tier localisations first.

So "POSSIBLE for L7-S3" tells the vet: do not forget L7-S3, but peripheral nerve is the more likely answer. "PROBABLE for L7-S3" tells the vet: treat L7-S3 as a real differential alongside peripheral; weight your workup accordingly. The operational difference is real — it changes which imaging / diagnostics the vet is likely to order first.

What your reply implies

Your message said: "I would even say L7-S3 probable, at least possible, for monoparesis of pelvic limb because the lesions tend to be much more inside the vertebral canal rather than the nerve itself."

Read literally, that nudges the rule from POSSIBLE to PROBABLE — the reasoning being that the prior probability of a canal-level lesion is higher than a peripheral-nerve lesion in your clinical experience. We are happy to encode that, but it changes the rule's output for any case that matches the pattern, and it changes the expected result of one of the existing tests.
