Proposition — joint venture, year 1

From: Dario · For: Ivana, Ronaldo · Date: May 2026 · Status: draft for discussion

This is the first written version of the go-to-market deal. Read it as a starting point for the call, not a final term sheet.

Where we are now

  • The localisation engine is complete: 99/99 of Ivana's tests pass on a deterministic rule set, with diagnoses scored per region and tiered (HIGHLY LIKELY → ESSENTIALLY EXCLUDED). The output is grounded — an AI agent placed on top of it cannot hallucinate a localisation or a differential outside of what the engine produces.
  • The Parameters and Localisation apps are usable end-to-end on a desktop. What we have is a working POC, not a product.
  • Ronaldo has 34k followers across two Instagram accounts, predominantly practicing vets — a potential never used for selling a digital product.

What I am proposing

Structure
  • Year 1: Bemjax stake = 10% equity + €2,000 / month (+ applicable tax).
  • Year 2: Bemjax stake = 15% equity + €2,000 / month (+ applicable tax) — additional 5% vested for the second year of commitment.
  • The €2k/month is permanent — not a year-one promo. It funds the Bemjax delivery side on an ongoing basis (see "What the €2k covers" below).
  • Our commitment: all of the work in the table above, plus advising on what Ivana and Ronaldo do operationally. Bemjax acts as the agency, with several people on the team contributing.
  • Your commitment: Ivana executes the conversion playbook on the Instagram audience and runs day-to-day product/content. Ronaldo provides domain validation, occasional source recordings (for content scaling), and is the face of the brand.
  • Year-one target: 200 paying accounts at ~$10/month — break-even on the Bemjax fee. Anything above that is profit to be split per equity.
  • Year-one outcome: a published, bootstrapped, self-sufficient product. From there it scales worldwide on its own funnel, or we open it to a vet-oriented VC.

What the €2k covers (and what it does not)

The €2k/month is an all-in agency rate for Bemjax. It covers everything we deliver:

  • All development work — Dario and the rest of the Bemjax team.
  • Infrastructure — hosting, database, monitoring, email, CDN, certificates.
  • Tooling on the funnel side — landing page, email platform, analytics.
  • App store accounts — Apple Developer, Google Play.
  • Domains, legal templates, baseline brand assets.
  • Strategy and advisory across the year.

The only cost outside the €2k is the AI API bill — the LLM that powers the agent layer wrapping the engine. It is variable because it scales with the number of cases run and the prompt volume per case. We track it monthly and pass it through at cost — no markup.

The €2k figure is net of tax — applicable VAT (or equivalent in the chosen jurisdiction) is added on top per invoice.

Discovery work — things we have to figure out before we lock anything

A non-trivial slice of year one is investigation, not building. None of us has shipped a paid digital product into the Brazilian market first before, and several decisions cannot be made right now, we just dont know facts or have numbers.

AreaWhat we have to find out
Procedural — incorporation jurisdiction Where do we register the legal entity? Three live candidates:
  • Croatia / EU — easiest for me to operate, EU banking, Stripe-friendly. Tax treatment of payouts to Ivana / Ronaldo in Brazil needs to be checked.
  • Brazil — natural for a BR-first product, easier for Ivana / Ronaldo, but the highest administrative overhead. CNPJ, accountant on retainer, currency exchange friction on USD revenue.
  • US (Delaware C-Corp or LLC) — standard for software / VC track, frictionless Stripe + Apple / Google payouts. Foreign-shareholder admin overhead for all three of us.
Decision driver: where revenue lands, how dividends or salaries flow, what a future VC round would prefer.
Brazilian legal frame We have no direct experience here. Open questions:
  • LGPD specifics — Data Protection Officer requirement at our scale, where data must reside, breach-notification timing.
  • CRMV classification of "diagnostic decision support" — already on the legal TODO (see legal.html). If they classify it as a medical device, year one shifts substantially.
  • Consumer-protection rules (CDC) — refund windows on subscriptions, cancellation rights, what we must say in the checkout flow.
  • Tax on cross-border SaaS — IOF, withholding, ISS at municipal level if BR entity. Whether US / EU entity changes this.
  • Marketing rules around veterinary products and AI-generated content — anything we publish through marketing tools and services needs to be clean against any current or pending rule.
Operational — paid accounts & vendors By launch we will be running ~10+ paid services: domains (.com, .com.br), hosting, database, mail, monitoring, AI API, marketing tools and services, Stripe, Apple Developer, Google Play, analytics, error tracking, etc. Several of these auto-debit a card and refuse to keep service when payment fails. We need this frictionless from day one:
  • One business card or virtual-card setup tied to the legal entity, not a personal card.
  • Centralised vendor inventory — what is paid, on which card, when it renews, who else has access.
Banking & revenue routing Not a day-one blocker. The €2k/month covers the first six months of operating costs without any revenue moving through the system, so Stripe / Apple / Google payout routing, FX cost, any holding-vs-operating structure can be decided closer to actual revenue.
IP & ownership clarity The localisation engine and the codebase have to be assigned to the legal entity from day one, not held personally. The Bemjax-as-agency relationship to the entity needs to be in writing — service contract, IP assignment, no ambiguity later - to Ronaldo I guess.

What "200 accounts at $10" actually means

200 paying accounts × $10/month = $2,000/month gross. That covers the €2k/month Bemjax fee at parity (give or take currency). It does not cover server costs, AI API costs, app store fees (~30% on mobile), or Ivana's time. So 200 accounts is the floor — the point at which we know the funnel works and the product retains. The actual sustainability target is somewhere between 300 and 500 accounts.

Pricing of $10 is illustrative. Final pricing comes out of user conversations and competitor research before launch — see pricing.html.

The Instagram funnel — the leverage

The reason this is doable in a year and on a small budget is that we are not buying ads or hiring a marketing agency. We have 30k+ vets who already follow Ronaldo. We need roughly 200 of them to convert — under 1%. That is a realistic ask if the content is right and the product earns trust. Most of Ivana's strategic work in year one is running that funnel.

The plan for the funnel — pre-launch teasers, soft launch via early-bird offer (Ronaldo, on a TikTok video shows the app), case-walkthrough content, AI-clone content scaling — is in content.html.

What this is not

  • We dont need an investemnt other than this. We are bootstrapping.
  • There is no ad spend in year one, for marketing.
  • This is not a feature factory. We launch a focused product and iterate on real-user feedback. A long feature wishlist before launch is the most common way to fail.
  • This is not exclusive to year one. If we reach 200 by month 9, we re-discuss structure for year two from a position of strength.
