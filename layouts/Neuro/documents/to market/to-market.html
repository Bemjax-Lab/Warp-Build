2. Pricing Strategy

Decide before launch

Pricing is TBD. The values below ($2–30/month) are an initial testing range only. Actual pricing must be determined through market research, competitor analysis, user interviews, and testing. Key factors: willingness to pay in target markets (Brazil vs. international), perceived value of the tool, cost of alternatives, and operational costs (servers, API calls, support).
OptionTest RangeNotes
Monthly$2–30/monthWide range — needs validation with real users
AnnualTBDTypically 15–20% discount vs. monthly
Founding MemberTBD (lower end of range)Locked-in price to reward early adopters
Free trial7–14 daysRemoves friction; vet tries on a real case, gets value, converts

How to determine the right price

  • Talk to target users: Ask 10–20 vets "what would you pay monthly for this?" — don't guess
  • Research competitors: What do VIN, Plumb's, VetBloom, and similar tools charge?
  • Consider markets separately: Brazilian vets may have different willingness to pay than US/EU vets — consider regional pricing in BRL
  • Factor in costs: Hosting, AI API costs per query, support time, app store fees (30%)
  • Test before committing: You can A/B test pricing on the landing page before the app even launches
  • Start and adjust: It's easier to lower a price than to raise one — but don't anchor too low
  • All Decide final price point
    • Ask 5–10 vet colleagues: "would you pay $X/month for this?" — test $19, $29, $39
    • Research what competitors charge (VetBloom, VIN, Plumb's — all $30+/month)
    • Factor in Brazil vs. international pricing (consider BRL pricing for BR market)
  • All Decide on pricing tiers structure
    • Option A: single tier (simplest — recommended for launch)
    • Option B: free limited + paid full (risk: free tier cannibalizes paid)
    • Decide: monthly only, or monthly + annual discount?
  • All Define the "founding member" offer
    • Locked-in price: $19/month forever? Or for 12 months?
    • How many founding member spots? (50? 100? creates scarcity)
    • What's the deadline to claim it?
  • All Define free trial terms
    • Duration: 7 or 14 days?
    • Require credit card upfront? (higher conversion but fewer signups)
    • Full access or limited during trial?
  • Dario/Bemjax Implement payment system (see App Store section)
Pricing page ready, founding member offer defined, payment flow tested

5. Legal & Compliance

Before launch

Do not skip these. Skipping legal setup can get the app pulled from stores or create liability.
  • Dario/Bemjax Add in-app disclaimer
    • Text: "This is a diagnostic aid tool. It does not replace clinical judgment, physical examination, or complementary exams."
    • Must be visible: on first launch (accept screen), in app footer/about, on landing page
    • User must explicitly accept before using the app
  • Dario/Bemjax Create Terms of Service
    • Use a generator (Termly.io, TermsFeed) as starting point — costs ~$10–50
    • Key clauses: limitation of liability, no guarantee of diagnostic accuracy, subscription terms, cancellation policy
    • Must be in Portuguese for Brazilian users
    • Host at yoursite.com/terms
  • Dario/Bemjax Create Privacy Policy (LGPD-compliant)
    • What data is collected (email, usage data, case data entered)
    • How data is stored and protected
    • User rights: access, deletion, portability (LGPD requirement)
    • Whether any data is shared with third parties (analytics, payment processor)
    • Cookie policy if web-based
    • Host at yoursite.com/privacy
  • Ronaldo Check veterinary regulatory requirements
    • Contact CRMV (Conselho Regional de Medicina Veterinaria) — ask if a "diagnostic decision support app" requires registration
    • Check MAPA requirements for veterinary software
    • "Decision support tool" is typically NOT classified as a medical device — confirm this
    • Document the response for your records
  • All Decide on business entity
    • Who owns the app legally? Personal? LLC? Partnership?
    • How is revenue split between team members?
    • Even a simple written agreement between founders is essential before money comes in
  • Ronaldo Review all user-facing text for clinical accuracy
    • App descriptions, marketing copy, disclaimers
    • Ensure no claims that could be seen as "diagnosing" — always "assisting", "suggesting", "supporting"
Terms of Service live, Privacy Policy live, disclaimer in app, regulatory status confirmed, founder agreement signed

6. Content Scaling — Ronaldo's Digital Clone

Week 3–6

The problem: Ronaldo is the brand, but his time is extremely limited (clinical work, teaching, travel). Content creation — especially video — is essential for marketing but is a bottleneck. The solution: create an AI-powered digital clone of Ronaldo to produce video content at scale, managed by Ivana.

What is a digital clone?

An AI-generated video avatar that looks and sounds like Ronaldo, capable of delivering scripted content (case explanations, app tutorials, educational tips) without Ronaldo needing to be on camera every time. Ivana writes the scripts, the AI generates the video.

    • Video: 2–5 minutes of him talking directly to camera, good lighting, neutral background
    • Voice: 10–30 minutes of clean audio (reading scripts or talking naturally)
    • Multiple angles/expressions if tool supports it
    • This is a one-time recording session (~1 hour of Ronaldo's time)
  • Ivana Choose and set up the AI avatar platform
    • Test HeyGen and Synthesia with free trials first
    • Key criteria: Portuguese language support, lip-sync quality, how "real" it looks

    • Does it represent him accurately?
    • Is the voice convincing?
    • Is he comfortable with it being used publicly?
  • Ivana Create content production workflow

    • Options: fully disclose ("powered by AI"), subtle mention in bio, or mix AI + real videos
    • Recommendation: be transparent — "content created with AI, clinical knowledge by Dr. Ronaldo"
    • The vet community values authenticity — dishonesty here could backfire badly
