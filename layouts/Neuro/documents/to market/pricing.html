Pricing strategy

Pricing is TBD until launch. The values below are a working range, not commitments.
Pricing is the single decision we should not guess. The right number comes from talking to vets, not from spreadsheets. Plan: open the launch around $10/month with an early-bird discount for the first cohort, then adjust within 3–6 months based on real conversion and retention data.

Working range

OptionTest rangeNotes
Monthly subscription$8–25 / monthWide. Plumb's, VIN, VetBloom sit at $30+. We come in below for soft launch, raise post-feedback.
Annual subscription15–20% off the monthlyImproves cash flow, reduces churn. Worth offering from day 1.
Early bird / frienddiscount on the launch price for the first cohortDiscount level and cohort size decided closer to launch — rewards the people who try it first.
Free trial7 or 14 daysRemoves friction. Vet tries it on a real case.
Free tierNone recommendedFree tier risks cannibalising paid. The trial is enough.

How we land on the number

  1. Talk to 10–20 target vets before launch. Not "would you pay?" — "what would you pay monthly for this?" Open ended. Note the floor and ceiling.
  2. Research competitors. What do VIN, Plumb's, VetBloom, regional Brazilian alternatives charge? Where do we sit?
  3. Factor in costs. App store cut (30% mobile), Stripe (~3% web), AI API per query, hosting. The web price can be lower than the mobile price for the same product.
  4. Region-segment. Brazil pricing in BRL with regional purchasing power in mind. International in USD or EUR.
  5. A/B test on the landing page before the app launches — show two prices to two visitor groups, count signups for each.
  6. Start, watch, adjust. Easier to lower a price than to raise one. Do not anchor too low to be safe.

Web vs mobile pricing

Apple and Google take 30% on in-app purchases. Stripe (web) takes ~3%. That is a 27 percentage-point gap. We do not absorb that gap as margin loss — we price web slightly cheaper and let the user choose.

ChannelCutSuggested headline priceNet to us
Web (Stripe)~3%$10 / month~$9.70
iOS / Android (in-app)30%$13 / month~$9.10

Net is comparable; the user gets a small "save by paying on the web" nudge; we comply with both stores' rules. (Apple has loosened "steering" rules but the safe pattern is still: never link out to web payments from inside the iOS app.)

Early-bird tier — what it is for

  • Reward early supporters. The first vets to try it in real cases are the ones who will tell others. They get a discount.
  • Cohort signal. An early cohort tells us whether we have a 30%, 5%, or 0.5% conversion of warm Instagram traffic. That number drives everything for year one.
Recommended launch shape
  • 14-day free trial, no credit card upfront (lower conversion to paid but more usage signal)
  • $10 / month web, $13 / month mobile
  • $96 / year web (20% off), $130 / year mobile
  • Early-bird discount on launch price — discount level and cohort size decided closer to launch
  • BR regional pricing in BRL at ~70% of USD equivalent

What changes after the first 100 paying users

  • If conversion is > 1% of warm IG traffic, raise the standard price toward $15.
  • If retention is below 70% at 30 days, do not touch price — fix the product.
  • If a clinic asks for "team licences" — note it, do not build it. Year-two work.
  • If a region (US, EU, AU) shows organic interest, add a regional price tier with proper localisation.
