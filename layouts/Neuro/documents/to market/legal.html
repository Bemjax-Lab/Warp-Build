Disclaimer language

Suggested wording (PT and EN versions both required):

"This is a diagnostic aid tool. It does not replace clinical judgment, physical examination, or complementary diagnostic exams. The veterinarian using this tool remains fully responsible for all clinical decisions."

"Esta é uma ferramenta de auxílio diagnóstico. Não substitui o julgamento clínico, o exame físico nem exames complementares. O médico-veterinário que utiliza esta ferramenta permanece integralmente responsável por todas as decisões clínicas."

This wording must be visible:

  • On first app launch — user must explicitly accept before continuing
  • In the app footer / about screen — always reachable
  • On the landing page
  • In the Terms of Service

Terms of Service — key clauses

  • Limitation of liability. No guarantee of diagnostic accuracy. The user accepts that the tool is supportive, not authoritative.
  • Subscription terms. Billing cycle, price, currency. How and when charges occur.
  • Trial-to-paid conversion. Explicit — user is told exactly when the first charge will happen.
  • Cancellation. User can cancel at any time. Cancellation effective at end of current billing period. No partial refunds (standard SaaS).
  • Refunds. Per local law (consumer rights in BR / EU); be ready to honour the legal minimum without arguing.
  • Permitted use. Tool is for licensed veterinarians. Not for animal owners self-diagnosing pets. Not for human medicine under any circumstance.
  • Account suspension. Conditions under which we may suspend (abuse, scraping, sharing accounts).
  • Governing law. Whichever jurisdiction the legal entity is registered in.

Privacy Policy — LGPD specifics

Brazilian users are subject to LGPD (similar to GDPR). The policy must list:

  • What data is collected — email, name, usage analytics, case data the user enters
  • Purposes of processing — service provision, billing, support, product improvement
  • Legal basis for processing each category
  • Storage location and security measures (encryption at rest and in transit)
  • Third parties involved — payment processor (Stripe), analytics, AI API providers
  • User rights — access, correction, deletion, portability, withdrawal of consent
  • Retention period — how long we keep data after account closure (90 days is a common floor)
  • Data Protection Officer contact (LGPD requires a designated contact)
  • How to exercise rights — email or in-app form
Case data entered by the vet is the most sensitive category. Default position: case data is stored only against the vet's own account, never used for AI model training without explicit opt-in, never shared with third parties.

Regulatory question — is this a "medical device"?

Decision-support tools for licensed practitioners are typically not classified as medical devices in Brazil (Anvisa) or the EU (MDR), as long as the tool does not directly determine treatment without a practitioner in the loop. The wording "decision support" + "clinical judgment remains with the veterinarian" matters here.

That said: do not assume. Ronaldo contacts CRMV in writing to confirm the classification, and we keep the response on file. If the response is "this is a medical device", year-one plan changes substantially and we have to discuss before launching.

Founder agreement — what it covers

Roles & responsibilitiesWho owns what (matches the table in plan.html)
IP ownershipThe localisation engine, the brand, the codebase — owned by the legal entity, not individuals
Decision rulesWhat requires unanimous decision (price changes, partnerships, structural changes), what is delegated (day-to-day product, content)
VestingBemjax's year-1 10% vests over the 12 months (e.g. monthly), not granted upfront. Year-2 5% escalation vests across year 2 on the same monthly cadence. Protects everyone if someone walks early.
Exit clausesWhat happens if a founder leaves voluntarily / is removed / cannot continue. Buyout terms.
Year-end reviewWhat happens at month 12 — restructure, raise, wind down. Defined in the agreement, not negotiated under pressure.
Dispute resolutionMediation first, then defined jurisdiction. Avoid open-ended litigation clauses.
This is the one document we should pay a lawyer for, even if everything else is template-based. Budget €200–500 for review. Founder disputes are the most common reason early-stage products die — and they only happen when nothing was written down.

Legal order of operations

  1. Founder agreement signed → before money flows. Not after.
  2. Privacy Policy + Terms live on a public URL → before any app store submission, before first marketing email.
  3. CRMV confirmation received → before public launch (not blocking PWA pre-launch tests with a closed group).
