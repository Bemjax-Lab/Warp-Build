Apple Developer enrolment — DUNS first

The DUNS number is the gating step. If we register the developer account as a company / legal entity, Apple requires a Dun & Bradstreet (DUNS) number tied to the entity. Getting one takes 1–2 weeks (free in the EU / US; intermediaries charge). Start this the day the legal entity is set up — see proposition.html for the incorporation question.
  • Alternative: enrol as individual first to skip DUNS, then transfer the app to a company account once the entity exists. Adds an Apple-side transfer dance later (1 working day).
  • Two-factor authentication required on the Apple ID used for enrolment.
  • Verified phone number for the enrolment contact.
  • Bank account in the entity's country for paid-app payouts (subscription revenue lands here).
  • Tax forms — W-8BEN-E if non-US, plus the country's local tax ID for the App Store payouts setup.

The big constraint — no steering to web payments

Apple's "anti-steering" rules have loosened (the 2024 Epic ruling, the EU DMA), but the safe pattern is still: do not link out to web payments from inside the iOS app. Mention pricing, yes; "tap here to pay on the web" — no. The rules vary by region (EU is more permissive after DMA). Default to the strictest interpretation until we have legal review on the regional variants.

Pricing implication: web price is lower than iOS price (3% Stripe vs 30% Apple cut). Users discover this from the website or pre-purchase email — not from a link inside the iOS app. See pricing.html for the suggested price split.

iOS-specific rejection patterns

PatternFix
Guideline 4.2 — "Minimum Functionality"An app that "looks like a website wrapper" gets rejected. The PWA wrapper alone is not enough — the iOS app needs to feel native (StoreKit IAP, push notifications, biometric auth, share sheet integration).
Guideline 5.1.1(v) — Subscription disclosureThe price, billing frequency, and "auto-renewing" must appear on the same screen as the Subscribe button. Not buried in T&C.
Guideline 3.1.1 — In-App PurchaseDigital subscriptions sold inside the app must use StoreKit, not Stripe. No exception.
Guideline 1.4.1 — Medical claimsApple is twitchy about medical apps. "Aid", "support", "decision support" — fine. "Diagnoses", "treats", "cures" — rejected.
Reviewer cannot complete a caseDemo account login fails, backend errors during review, or sample data is missing. We test the demo account on a clean device 24h before submission.

Order of operations — iOS

  1. Legal entity exists → start DUNS (1–2 weeks).
  2. DUNS received → enrol Apple Developer Program ($99 charged on enrolment).
  3. App record created → bundle ID registered → subscription products configured.
  4. Native iOS wrapper around the PWA built (or fully native if scope allows). StoreKit subscription flow tested on a real device with sandbox accounts.
  5. Server-to-server notification endpoint live and persisting subscription state.
  6. Privacy Policy URL stable (see legal.html).
  7. App Store Connect submission — assets, descriptions, demo account credentials.
  8. First review (1–3 days). Expect 1–2 rejection rounds; budget 2 weeks total.
  9. Approval → release scheduled (manual release, not auto, so we coordinate with launch content).
