Google Play–specific publishing. Common requirements live in publish-common.html.

Google Play Console — what to set up

StepDetail
Developer account$25 one-time. Identity verification is required as of late 2023 — passport / ID + selfie. Adds 1–7 days.
Payments profileBank account in the entity's country, tax info. Required before publishing a paid app or subscription.
Application createdApp record in Play Console. Package name (com.<entity>.<app>) is permanent — same as iOS bundle ID for consistency.
Closed testing trackInternal / closed / open testing tracks let us push builds without going through full review. Use this for the first 1–2 weeks of real-device testing.
Subscription productSet up in Play Console. Define base plan (monthly / annual), free trial offer, early-bird offer. Pricing tiers automatically converted by Google to local currencies.
Real-time developer notificationsGoogle publishes subscription state-change events to a Pub/Sub topic; our backend subscribes and persists state.
Google Play Billing LibraryLatest version (v7+). Integration docs. Integrates the IAP / subscription purchase flow into the app.
License keyGenerated in Play Console; used by our backend when verifying purchase signatures.

Steering to web — Google is more relaxed than Apple

Google Play's "User Choice Billing" pilot (and the EU DMA) lets apps offer an alternative billing method alongside Google Play Billing in some regions, with a reduced (typically 11–26%) Play cut. Eligibility varies by region and developer program. For year one we keep it simple: Google Play Billing for purchases inside the Android app, Stripe for purchases on the web. We do not link out from the Android app to web payments (matches the iOS pattern — keeps cross-platform behaviour symmetric and removes one rejection vector).

Android-specific rejection / removal patterns

PatternFix
Identity verification incompleteComplete ID + selfie verification before submitting. Apps from unverified developers are blocked from publishing.
Missing data safety formThe Data Safety section in Play Console must be filled in — every data category collected and how it is used. Inconsistencies with the Privacy Policy get flagged.
Permissions not justifiedSensitive permissions (location, contacts, storage) need a clear justification in the listing. We avoid them entirely; the app does not need them.
Medical claims in store descriptionSame as iOS — "decision support", "diagnostic aid". Never "diagnoses", "treats", "cures".
Subscription billing surpriseFree trial → first charge must be clearly disclosed. Google audits this; auto-renew language must be on the same screen as the Subscribe button.

Order of operations — Android

  1. Legal entity exists → developer account creation ($25) → identity verification (1–7 days).
  2. Payments profile filled in (bank, tax info).
  3. App record created → package name registered → subscription products configured.
  4. Native Android wrapper around the PWA built. Google Play Billing flow tested on a real device with a closed-testing track.
  5. Real-time developer notification endpoint live, persisting subscription state.
  6. Privacy Policy URL + Data Safety form filled in (see legal.html).
  7. Submission to closed testing → internal validation → promote to production.
  8. First review for production track — typically hours to days.
